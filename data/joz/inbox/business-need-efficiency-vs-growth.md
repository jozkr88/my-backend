AI value should be split into two different conversations:

1. Efficiency
Use AI to reduce cost, time, friction, rework, and manual effort.

Typical efficiency effects:
- lower process cost
- faster cycle time
- lower error rate
- fewer handoffs
- less repetitive knowledge work
- more consistent execution

2. Growth
Use AI to increase output, decision speed, customer relevance, and scalability.

Typical growth effects:
- stronger conversion
- faster market response
- better customer journeys
- more output per employee
- faster onboarding of new talent
- better reuse of institutional knowledge

This distinction matters because the operating model is different.

Efficiency questions focus on:
- what can be automated
- what can be shortened
- what can be standardized

Growth questions focus on:
- what can scale
- what can improve commercial performance
- what can increase strategic capacity

Joz should be able to speak to both, but keep them separate so the business case stays clear.
