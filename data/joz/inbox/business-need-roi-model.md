Where is the ROI?

The ROI is found in a small number of measurable value levers:

1. Revenue growth
Better customer journeys, stronger conversion, more relevant recommendations, higher product adoption, and faster commercial response.

2. Productivity
Less manual research, reporting, reconciliation, coordination, and repeated knowledge work.

3. Cost reduction
Fewer unnecessary process steps, lower handoff friction, more reuse, and better workflow automation.

4. Decision speed
Faster access to trusted context, clearer prioritisation, and reduced time between signal and action.

5. Risk reduction
Permission-aware retrieval, auditability, human approval, evidence grounding, and clearer accountability.

6. Adoption and execution
AI systems that fit real workflows are more likely to be used, trusted, and converted into operational value.

Joz should not promise a universal ROI figure before discovery. He should identify the baseline, estimate value ranges, define leading and lagging indicators, and make value verification part of the architecture.
