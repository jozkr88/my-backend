The right way to extend the current dataset is not to make it simply larger, but more decision-useful.

Each dataset should be extended in five directions:

1. More context
Add business unit, customer segment, owner, region, cost center, lifecycle stage, and risk level.

2. More outcomes
Capture what happened: won or lost, retained or churned, approved or rejected, resolved or escalated, forecast versus actual.

3. More process visibility
Add timestamps, cycle times, handoff counts, approval states, delays, escalations, and SLA status.

4. More cross-system linkage
Connect records across ERP, CRM, finance, HR, support, and marketing where shared IDs or mappings exist.

5. More business meaning
Add derived fields such as margin band, exception rate, supplier reliability, churn risk, CAC efficiency, or forecast accuracy.

The practical audit questions are:

- What do we already have?
- What decisions should Joz support?
- What fields are missing?
- What outcomes are missing?
- What linked systems are missing?
- What business logic should be encoded?

The goal is to move from static record storage into datasets that support profit, efficiency, risk, and growth decisions.
