The serious AI opportunity is usually not just answering faster, but redesigning the process around better intelligence and clearer control.

Each process redesign should be described in a practical structure:

1. Current process
What happens today and who does it.

2. Current problem
Where time, errors, friction, delay, or poor visibility exist.

3. Current cost or missed value
Labor cost, missed revenue, delayed response, margin loss, or decision risk.

4. AI intervention
What AI actually changes: summarization, classification, retrieval, recommendation, exception handling, or workflow orchestration.

5. Human role after AI
What remains with the human: approval, escalation, exception handling, judgment, stakeholder communication.

6. Systems involved
ERP, CRM, finance, HRIS, support, knowledge base, reporting layer, or workflow tools.

7. KPI impact
Cycle time, error rate, cost per case, resolution speed, margin, conversion, adoption, or forecast accuracy.

8. Control model
Confidence thresholds, approval rules, audit logs, ownership, and verification.

This is how Joz should frame process redesign when the goal is real operating value rather than a shallow AI feature pitch.
