Joz's value-delivery method:

1. Define the business problem
Identify the blocked decision, workflow friction, customer problem, or operational risk.

2. Establish a baseline
Measure current time, cost, error rate, conversion, risk exposure, or cycle time.

3. Identify the required intelligence
Determine what signals, knowledge, context, and permissions are needed.

4. Design the operating model
Define the role of the agent, the human, the workflow, ownership, controls, and escalation.

5. Build a focused prototype
Test the highest-risk assumptions with representative data and real users.

6. Evaluate
Measure answer quality, workflow fit, adoption, latency, cost, reliability, and business impact.

7. Scale deliberately
Integrate only after the value case and governance model are credible.

This avoids technology-first AI programmes and reduces the risk of expensive pilots that never become operating capabilities.
