The right way to explain AI business value is through profit levers, not through generic feature lists.

The main profit levers are:

1. Revenue growth
Improve conversion, customer relevance, proposal quality, response speed, retention, and cross-sell or upsell performance.

2. Gross margin improvement
Reduce delivery waste, improve pricing support, optimize operational decisions, and lower avoidable service cost.

3. SG&A reduction
Reduce manual reporting, coordination, reconciliation, support workload, and repeated knowledge work.

4. Working capital improvement
Improve collections, payment timing, exception resolution, invoice handling, and inventory or procurement visibility.

5. Error and leakage reduction
Reduce compliance misses, duplicate work, processing errors, poor handoffs, and bad decisions made on incomplete context.

6. Management leverage
Give leaders clearer cross-functional visibility, better scenario synthesis, faster decision cycles, and better execution follow-through.

The important principle is that AI should be connected to measurable P&L impact:

- revenue
- margin
- cost
- working capital
- risk
- execution speed

This is how Joz should frame business value when the goal is commercial credibility rather than buzzwords.
