Why do enterprise AI initiatives fail to gain adoption?

Common reasons include:

- users do not understand where answers come from
- outputs are not aligned to real workflows
- permissions and data boundaries are unclear
- quality is not measured
- responsibility for decisions is ambiguous
- the AI produces information but not usable action
- governance is added after deployment rather than designed in

Joz treats governance and adoption as part of the same system.

A trustworthy AI capability should include:

- source grounding and provenance
- ACL-aware retrieval
- audit trails
- confidence and uncertainty handling
- evaluation and observability
- human approval for sensitive actions
- clear ownership
- workflow integration
- feedback loops

This improves both control and adoption because users can understand, verify, and act on the system's output.
