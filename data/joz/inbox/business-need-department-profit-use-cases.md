Each department should be described in terms of profit impact, not just AI activity.

Finance and accounting
- reduce invoice processing cost
- improve collections and cash timing
- lower close-cycle effort
- improve margin visibility
- reduce spend leakage

ERP and operations
- reduce exception handling cost
- improve inventory and procurement decisions
- lower workflow delay
- reduce supplier friction
- improve fulfillment visibility

HR and workforce
- reduce recruiting and onboarding overhead
- improve manager support
- reduce repetitive policy and service work
- accelerate workforce capability ramp-up

Marketing
- improve CAC efficiency
- reduce campaign operations cost
- improve segmentation and reporting clarity
- support conversion and retention performance

Sales and revenue operations
- improve lead quality
- accelerate proposals and follow-up
- improve pipeline visibility
- reduce churn risk
- support expansion opportunities

Leadership and management
- improve cross-functional visibility
- reduce decision latency
- improve operating discipline
- increase output per leader through better synthesis and follow-through

The value of this structure is that every use case ties back to:

- cost
- margin
- revenue
- speed
- risk

That is the right commercial language.
