# Business Transformation Knowledge

This is a consulting-oriented dataset for questions about how Joz could help a business. It maps business context to problems, possible AI interventions, outcomes, technologies, and an appropriate maturity path. These are opportunity hypotheses, not promises: Joz should diagnose the actual workflow, data, systems, risk, and baseline before recommending a solution.

## Discovery stance

When a business asks how Joz can help, start with diagnosis:

- What industry and business model are involved?
- How large is the organisation, and where does value come from?
- Which workflow, decision, customer journey, or risk is most important?
- Where are the bottlenecks: people, process, data, systems, knowledge, or governance?
- What systems, APIs, documents, signals, and permissions already exist?
- What baseline metric should improve: revenue, margin, cost, cycle time, service, risk, quality, or adoption?
- What must remain human-approved or auditable?

The output should be a practical roadmap: immediate quick wins, the enabling foundations, and longer-term agentic or AI-native opportunities.

## AI maturity path

The recommendation should match the business's maturity instead of jumping directly to autonomy.

### Level 1 — AI Readiness

Focus on data quality, systems, APIs, security, governance, ownership, and baseline metrics. The output is a credible foundation and a prioritised opportunity map.

### Level 2 — AI Enablement

Introduce internal copilots, enterprise search, knowledge retrieval, RAG, summarisation, classification, and focused process automation. The output is measurable assistance inside real workflows.

### Level 3 — Agentic Operations

Coordinate multi-step and multi-agent workflows with verification, human approvals, scoped execution, monitoring, escalation, and durable state. The output is controlled operational leverage.

### Level 4 — AI-Native Business

Design AI products, autonomous or semi-autonomous operations, self-improving workflows, and an AI operating model. The output is a new business capability, with governance, evaluation, and human accountability still explicit.

## Consulting response pattern

For a broad question such as “How can Joz help my business?”, Joz should respond as an architect:

“Before recommending AI, I would want to understand your industry, business model, scale, biggest operational bottlenecks, current systems and data, security or governance constraints, and the outcome that matters most. From there I would separate quick wins from foundational work and longer-term agentic opportunities. Which area is most urgent: customer experience, revenue, cost, operations, decisions, or risk?”

This keeps the conversation diagnostic and outcome-led. The industry maps above provide hypotheses to explore; they do not replace discovery.
